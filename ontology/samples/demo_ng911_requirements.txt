The PSAP shall receive and process emergency calls in an NG911/i3 environment.
The system shall support Text-to-911 SMS emergency contacts.
Multimedia images and video shall be accepted to the PSAP per NG911 capabilities.
Caller location shall be acquired via ALI/AML civic and geodetic methods.
Emergency calls shall be routed via ESRP according to location and policy.
The solution shall query ECRF and validate location via LVF.
Abandoned and silent 911 calls shall be detected and processed.
TTY and real-time text (RTT) shall be supported for accessible emergency calling.
Language interpretation shall be conferenced for non-English emergency callers.
Emergency calls shall transfer and conference to secondary PSAPs.
A 911 call queue / ACD shall distribute calls across call-takers.
Emergency call recording with retention controls shall be provided.
Wireless Phase II handset location shall be acquired for mobile 911 callers.
Additional Data Repository (ADR) caller data shall display to call-takers.
PSAP failover and overflow to alternate PSAPs shall be supported.
EIDO incident data objects shall exchange with authorized functional elements.
Automatic crash notification / telematics shall deliver into the PSAP.
NG911 call data shall hand off into CAD for dispatch.
Location discrepancies between reported, ALI/AML, and verified locations shall be resolved.
Silent-call protocol shall apply staged verification for open-line 911 calls.
RTT transfer shall move real-time text sessions between PSAPs without dropping media.
NG911 multimedia retention policy shall retain image/video per legal hold.
ESRP congestion shall trigger alternate routing and overflow policies.
Legacy selective-router trunks shall bridge into the NG911 i3 call path.
Orphaned or stranded emergency sessions shall be recovered after FE failure.
Poison control specialty transfer shall retain caller location.
BCF shall enforce TLS signaling and SRTP media security.
Abandoned-call callback policy shall configure attempts, timers, and disposition.
